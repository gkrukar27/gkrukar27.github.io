Types Of Eczema

There are 7 main different types of eczema, with one other common type added to this list. These descriptions do not provide all-encompassing symptoms, but do provide a basis for anyone seeking an overview. Eczema commonly has these symptoms:

Dry, flaky skin
Itchiness
Small, raised bumps
Thickened skin
Raw, sensitive skin
Oozing and crusting
Pain in areas that are breaking out

Atopic Dermatitis

This causes inflammation, dryness, and itchy skin.

Contact Dermatitis

This form is brought on by coming into contact with allergens or external triggers.

Dyshidrotic Eczema

This dries out the skin and can cause burning, rashes, and blisters.

Neurodermatitis or Discoid Eczema

This makes small patches of skin itchy and scaly.

Nummular Eczema

This creates small, rounded lesions all over the body. This occurs mostly on arms and legs.

Seborrheic Dermatitis

This form causes your scalp to become inflamed.

Statis Dermatitis

Looking very similar to vericose veins, this type presents as skin discoloration on the legs.

Perioral Dermatitis

This type of eczema causes small blisters, similar to pimples, and scaly patches around the nose and mouth.
